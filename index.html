4-Dimensional Geometry

The Tesseract — a cube in 4D space

A tesseract is the four-dimensional analogue of a cube. Just as a cube unfolds into six squares, a tesseract unfolds into eight cubes. Drag and rotate to explore it.

What you're seeing

A shadow of the fourth dimension

Just as a 3D cube casts a 2D shadow, what you see here is a 3D projection of a 4D object. The inner and outer cubes are both the same size in four-dimensional space — perspective makes them appear different.

The animation rotates the tesseract simultaneously across multiple 4D planes: XY, XZ, XW, YW, and ZW — six possible planes of rotation in four dimensions, compared to just three in our world.

The dimensional ladder

From point to tesseract

0D
Point
A single location. No length, width, or height. 1 vertex, 0 edges.
1D
Line
Connect two points. Pure length. 2 vertices, 1 edge.
2D
Square
Sweep a line sideways. A flat surface. 4 vertices, 4 edges.
3D
Cube
Sweep a square upward. Volume. 8 vertices, 12 edges, 6 faces.
4D
Tesseract
Sweep a cube into 4D. 16 vertices, 32 edges, 24 faces, 8 cubic cells.
